PRL publishes results that are:
1. **Novel** - fundamentally new approach/discovery

3. **Surprising** - challenges existing paradigms
4. **Concise** - tells complete story in ~4 pages

## üöÄ Path to PRL Quality

### 1. **Strengthen the Physics Story**
- **Why does accuracy improve with N?** This is your hook! Investigate:
  - Is the neural operator learning collective/emergent behavior?
  - Does it exploit scaling laws that classical methods miss?
  - Can you prove this theoretically?

### 2. **Expand System Validation**
- Test on **different topologies**: 2D lattices, star graphs, random networks
- Try **different Hamiltonians**: disorder, long-range interactions
- Show **universality** - works across NMR/quantum computing/condensed matter

### 3. **Add Experimental Connection**
- Compare to **real experimental data** (published NMR spectra)
- Show your method predicts experimental observables better
- Demonstrate relevance to actual quantum devices

### 4. **Theoretical Insight**
- Derive **why** neural operators work here
- Connect to approximation theory, operator learning theory
- Show fundamental limits or scaling laws

### 5. **Benchmark Against State-of-the-Art**
Currently you compare to Krylov/Chebyshev. Also compare:
- Tensor networks (MPS/PEPS)
- Quantum circuit simulators
- Other ML methods (transformers, GNNs)
